Training Sequence Assignment in Uplink TDD Massive MIMO Systems

This thesis addresses the problem of channel estimation in multi-user interference limited cellular networks. Such networks deal with the multi-user interference by way of the limitation resource of training sequence. This problem would be more critical when the number of user become more and more such that some users should reuse the resource of training sequence and it would cause serious interference problem. In this thesis, we proposed a training-based channel estimation scheme with received beamforming method in time division duplex (TDD) multi-user large-scale multiple-antenna communication systems (so-called "massive" MIMO) in order to improve the channel estimation error. The proposed method appropriately arranged the training sequence for users and used the received beamforming method to reduce the inter-user-interference (IUI) or inter-symbol-interference (ISI). Simulation results show that the proposed method can reduce the channel estimation error and we further simulate the uplink transmission with CSIR at base station (BS) to verify our work.

Key words: Large-scale antenna, Time-division duplexing, Beamforming, Channel estimation, Training sequence
